Strawberry-Blueberry Pie

Equipment

·       Pastry cutter

·       Medium-large mixing bowl

·       Whisk

·       Spatula or mixing spoon

·       Plastic wrap or Ziplock bags

·       9” pie tin

·       Aluminum foil

Crusts

Ingredients

·       2 ½ cups flour

·       2 tsp sugar

·       1 tsp salt

·       ½ cup unsalted butter (keep cold in fridge until ready to use)

·       ½ cup vegetable shortening (keep cold in fridge until ready to use)

·       ½ cup ice water

Steps

1.       Whisk flour, sugar, and salt in mixing bowl.

2.       Cut butter into small slices or cubes.

3.       Add butter and shortening to dry mix and cut with pastry cutter until clumps of fat are pea-sized.

4.       Mix in water 1 tbsp at a time until dough comes together (there will be extra water).

5.       Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface and gently pack into a ball.

6.       Cut ball in half and flatten into two thick discs (like big hockey pucks).

7.       Wrap in plastic wrap or place in Ziplock bags and refrigerate for an hour.

8.       Clean and dry mixing bowl for later use.

Pie

Ingredients

·       2 pie crusts

·       4 cups sliced strawberries

·       2 cups blueberries

·       ¾ cup white sugar

·       6 tbsp flour

·       1 tsp lemon juice

·       1 egg, beaten

Steps

1.       Preheat oven to 400 degrees.

2.       Remove one dough disc from the fridge and roll out on a generously floured surface. Apply even pressure and roll from the inside out until it is about 12” in in diameter.

3.       Grease pie tin and place rolled-out bottom crust inside. Gently press in the dough until it touches the sides. Trim excess dough around edges and keep in fridge until ready to use.

4.       In mixing bowl, toss berries, flour, lemon juice, and sugar.

5.       Remove pie tin from fridge and fill bottom crust with berry filling.

6.       Remove 2nd dough disc from fridge and roll out in the same manner as the first crust.

7.       Cut out steam vent or vents in top crust (use any design you’d like).

8.       Lie top crust on top of filled pie and trim excess dough around edges. Crimp edges in any way you’d like (pressing the edges together with a fork is easy).

9.       Brush top crust with beaten egg and sprinkle with sugar.

10.   Loosely cover edges with aluminum foil to prevent them from browning too fast.

11.   Bake in middle rack for 45-50 minutes or until crust is golden brown.

12.   When crust is golden brown and filling has began bubbling, bake an additional 5-10 minutes.

13.   Allow pie to cool completely (1-2 hours) before cutting.
